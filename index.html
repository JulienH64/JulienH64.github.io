Introduction

League of Legends is a Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA) game created by Riot Games in 2009. The game boasts an average of 700,000 concurrent players daily and 112 million monthly players 12 years after release. League is a deceptively simple game with one objective: destroy the enemy team's "nexus tower". Each team competes head to head on an even stage in order to achieve that objective. In each game, there are optional objectives that teams can fight over in order to gain bonus stats to aide in capturing the final objective. With over 150 unique champions each with their own playstyle, there is something enjoyable for everyone.

Our motivation for analyzing factors behind win rate in League stems from how we are all active players of the game looking to have a deeper understanding of what affects a victory or loss. With the assistance of Riot's Data Dragon, game data from Korea can be extracted in order to analyze some of the quantitative factors of League games. An example of this is number of dragons, rift heralds, and barons collected by each team. This data shows games from the highest ranked players in the Korean Server over the full year of 2020.

In our tutorial, our goal is to read and tidy the data provided by Riot's API, process the data to determine which bonus objectives are most important in terms of contributing to a team's victory and be able to predict whether a not or team is likely to win given what bonus objectives they have accomplished at a certain point in the game. The aforementioned bonus objectives provide different benefits, such as giving a player the ability to summon a monster to help destroy turrets, giving every player on a team permanent buffs to various stats such as attack or health regeneration, or giving every player on a team a temporary ability to buff nearby minions. The difficulty of and appearance of these bonus objectives vary over time, so with our analysis we hope to help provide evidence for both new and old players on which bonus objectives are worth focusing on and to hopefully help players improve by allowing them to make better decisions regarding the various tradeoffs they can make.

  • QueueId: What type of queue the players used (Ranked Solo, Normal, One for All, etc.)
  • SeasonId: What season the game is played in
  • Status.message and Status.status_code which are both null

Tidying Data

For our analysis, we are interested in exploring the significance of the bonus objectives within the game and how important they are to obtaining victory. In addition, we want to specifically look at a single game mode type for consistency within our data. As a result, we will narrow our dataset to only look at classical ranked solo queue games on Summoner's Rift because it is the most common and popular game mode while also being competitive. To do this, we can look at Riot's API documentation to see that we can obtain the data we want by filtering for gameMode = CLASSIC and mapID = 11 for Summoner's Rift, and queueId = 420 for ranked solo queue. To keep the data even more consistent, we will also filter for the most recent version of the game using seasonId = 13 for season 2019.

Do Objectives affect Win Rate?¶

After determining that taking objectives has a correlation with win rate, one other factor that can affect win rate is game duration. Every major objective in league has a respawn timer of 5-8 minutes, so taking more objectives as the game goes on may skew results. In order to factor out game duration, the objective capture difference can be divided by the total number of each objective captured in order to give a ratio of objectives taken relatively each game, because taking 3 out of four dragons may have a different amount of significance compared to taking 5 out of 6 dragons.

The first step is to take the difference of each objective and divide it by the total objectives taken to get the ratio of objectives taken vs total objectives. The data is stored in a dataframe separate from the other data to keep analysis tidy.

We will first create a dataset from the perspective of the individual teams and then use their perspective to predict if they won. Since each game has a winner and loser, we will split the dataset into half of the winning perspective and half of the losing perspective.

Conclusion

From our analysis, we can see that the bonus objectives in League of Legends are tied very close to whether or not a team wins. We learned that getting Dragon and Rift Herald first is very beneficial, whereas getting Baron first is not as significant. Furthermore, we learned that for all three bonus objectives there is a clear benefit to always having more of these objectives than the opponent, especially Dragons.

Hopefully our tutorial will help shed some light on the importance of these bonus objectives to players, whether it be by emphasizing the significance of Dragon or deemphasizing the significance of Baron. For those interested in doing further analysis on this subject, we would recommend analyzing the timestamps for each objective. Unfortunately the data provided by the Kaggle dataset did not include this, but directly getting the data from Riot's API should contain this information. In addition for those that want to analyze the significance of other factors like team composition or gold difference, the Kaggle dataset does include this information. Our tutorial only touched the surface of what there is to look at, and we hope that reading it will make both old and new players more interested in playing the game.
